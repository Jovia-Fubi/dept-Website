🌟 Featured News

Department Receives Excellence in Research Award

March 10, 2026

The Department of Computing and Informatics has been honored with the prestigious Excellence in Research Award by the Uganda National Council for Science and Technology. This recognition comes as a result of our groundbreaking research in artificial intelligence applications for healthcare and agriculture.

The award ceremony, held at the Serena Hotel in Kampala, was attended by government officials, industry leaders, and academic representatives. Dr. John Mugisha, Head of Department, received the award on behalf of the department.

"This recognition validates our commitment to research that addresses real-world challenges facing our communities," said Dr. Mugisha during his acceptance speech.

New AI and Cybersecurity Programs Launch

March 15, 2026

We are excited to announce the launch of two new specialized programs: Bachelor of Science in Artificial Intelligence and Bachelor of Science in Cybersecurity. These programs will begin in August 2026 and aim to address the growing demand for skilled professionals in these critical fields.

The AI program will focus on machine learning, neural networks, and practical applications, while the Cybersecurity program will cover network security, ethical hacking, and digital forensics.

Student Team Wins National Hackathon

March 8, 2026

Congratulations to our student team "Code Warriors" for winning first place in the National University Hackathon 2026. The team developed an innovative mobile application for agricultural market information that helps rural farmers connect with buyers directly.

The team, consisting of four final-year students, competed against 25 teams from various universities across Uganda. Their solution impressed judges with its practical approach and technical implementation.

Industry Partnership with Microsoft Africa

February 28, 2026

We are pleased to announce a strategic partnership with Microsoft Africa to enhance our curriculum and provide students with access to cutting-edge technologies and certification opportunities. This collaboration includes curriculum development, faculty training, and student internship programs.

The partnership will focus on cloud computing, AI, and software development skills that are in high demand in the job market.

Faculty Publication in International Journal

February 20, 2026

Dr. Grace Achieng and Prof. Michael Okello have published a groundbreaking paper on "Machine Learning Applications for Disease Prediction in Resource-Limited Settings" in the prestigious International Journal of AI in Medicine.

The research, conducted over two years, demonstrates how AI can help predict disease outbreaks using limited healthcare data, potentially saving lives in rural communities.
